Inspection workflow

Guide technicians through a consistent, zone-by-zone inspection that captures the details you need for maintenance decisions.

Before you start

  • Confirm property, clock, and zone list is accurate. Update zone names if the landscape changed.
  • Review previous inspection issues and outstanding work orders.
  • Check weather restrictions and water district rules for the scheduled day.
  • Sync offline mode if the property has limited connectivity.

Technician checklist

  • Start inspection timer to log technician labor.
  • Capture meter reading (start/end) and backflow test status.
  • Run each zone manually and log observations, photos, and recommended actions.
  • Mark zones requiring repairs or quotes for follow-up scheduling.

Zone inspection entry

Required data

  • Location description: e.g., "Front lawn - east side".
  • Zone type: spray, rotor, drip, bubbler, etc.
  • Plant type: turf, shrubs, trees, annual beds, xeriscape.
  • Issues found: leaks, clogged nozzles, broken heads, coverage gaps, controller faults.

Optional data

  • Photos or video clips of problem areas.
  • Measured pressure and flow rate readings.
  • Immediate actions taken (e.g., swapped nozzle) with parts used.
  • Estimated labor/material cost for follow-up work orders.

Inspection completion

  • Summarize labor time, material used, and zones requiring quotes.
  • Generate PDF report with customer-friendly language and technician notes.
  • Send follow-up tasks to maintenance team or integrate with field service software.
  • Close the inspection to update property status and trigger customer notifications.
